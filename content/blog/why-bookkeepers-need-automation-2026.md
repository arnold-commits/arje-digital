If you are a bookkeeper or tax preparer still onboarding clients manually, you are leaving money on the table every single month.

## The math on manual processes

The average client onboarding takes 45 minutes when done manually. That includes sending the intake form, following up on missing documents, creating the client folder, setting up the tracking spreadsheet, and sending the welcome email.

At just 5 new clients per month, that is 3.75 hours. Over a year? Almost 45 hours — more than a full work week — spent copying and pasting the same information.

## What automation actually looks like

In my practice, a new client fills out a single JotForm. Everything else happens automatically:

- Google Drive folder is created with the right structure
- Welcome email sends with next steps
- Client tracking sheet gets updated
- I get a notification that a new client is ready

Total time on my end: zero. The client does the work of filling out the form, and the system handles everything else.

## The Google Workspace advantage

Everything in my system runs on free Google tools. Google Sheets, Google Docs, Google Drive, Google Forms. My clients do not need Microsoft Office. I do not pay for expensive practice management software.

Compare that to tools like Karbon or Canopy that charge $50-200 per month. My system costs zero dollars per month after the initial setup.
